## Purpose of the kit

This kit is the press-grade portrait collection of Olha Yevtushenko. It serves three audiences:

1. **Press features and interviews** — both online and print.
2. **Speaker bios** for conferences, panels, awards.
3. **Partnership communication** — partners and clients referencing the founder publicly.

Every portrait must work at full editorial standard — never as a Linkedin headshot equivalent.

---

## Visual direction

- Mirrou's signature lighting language — controlled gold beam (`#C6A56B`) against deep shadow (`#0A0A0A`) — extends into the portrait kit.
- Color palette: Deep Ground, Ivory, Mirrou Gold; no ambient pastel.
- Wardrobe palette: black, ivory, deep neutrals; quiet structural shapes; no logos.
- Hair and skin: editorial finish, never campaign-glossed.
- Mood: composed, present, slightly withholding. Not smiling for the camera; not performative.
- No props that suggest casual lifestyle. Optional studio props: notebook, lens, proof print, Leica.

**Color grading directive (applied in post):** lifted blacks, desaturated midtones, blues + warm gold highlights, grain overlay 5%.

---

## Required formats

- 4:5 vertical — primary editorial format, hero use.
- 1:1 square — social, profile, OG.
- 16:9 horizontal — speaker covers, banners.
- 2:3 vertical — print magazine standard.

Each setup should produce variants in at least two of these formats from the same lighting setup.

## Selection workflow

1. Tech-clean every selected raw file (color, dust, sensor spots).
2. Produce a contact sheet of 50–80 selects.
3. Internal review — narrow to 15–20.
4. Founder review (Olha) — final 8–15.
5. Final edit — color grading and retouching to editorial standard.
6. Export web + print versions.
7. Apply naming convention.
8. Add credit, caption and allowed-use to `CREDITS_AND_RIGHTS.csv`.
