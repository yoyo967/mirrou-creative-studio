# MIRROU — BRAND VOICE & TONE SHEET

**Last update:** 2026-05-09
**Audience:** Internal team + external partners writing on behalf of Mirrou Creative Studio
**Purpose:** Operating manual for everything written under the Mirrou name — press releases, captions, decks, web copy, social, internal comms.

---

## 1. POSITIONING

> "The personal eye of a star photographer, scaled through the logic of a studio."

Mirrou is a performance creative studio. Olha Yevtushenko is the artistic authority. Mirrou is the high-velocity engine that scales her vision into systems for clients.

The brand exists at the intersection of **personal art** (Olha) and **studio performance** (Mirrou).

---

## 2. BRAND ARCHETYPES

**The Magician** — transformation through knowledge.
**The Architect** — technical mastery in service of magical outcomes.

Together: *"Transformation through knowledge. Technical mastery to make the magical real."*

The voice should always feel like both — a magician explaining the trick, and an architect drawing the load-bearing wall.

---

## 3. TONALITY MATRIX

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Status | Authoritative |
| Temperature | Cool / Precise |
| Pace | Deliberate |
| Quality | Intellectual |

**Translation into writing:**
- Authoritative ≠ loud. We do not over-explain. We declare.
- Cool ≠ cold. We are precise, not detached.
- Deliberate ≠ slow. Every sentence is chosen, no filler.
- Intellectual ≠ academic. We respect the reader's intelligence.

---

## 4. HEADLINE ARCHETYPES (verbatim — release-cleared)

**The Personal Statement** — Olha's voice.
> "I don't just photograph products. I capture their soul's intention."

**The Studio Promise** — Mirrou's voice.
> "Mirrou is where aesthetics solve for performance."

**The Technical Truth** — the bridge between both.
> "Conversion is an emotional response to logical excellence."

Use these three archetypes whenever a Mirrou message needs an anchor line. Match the archetype to the audience: personal statement for editorial; studio promise for partners; technical truth for performance-led conversations.

---

## 5. FORBIDDEN LEXICON

Never use these terms in Mirrou copy. They violate the positioning.

- "Cheap" / "billig"
- "Quick fix"
- "Mass production"
- "Generic"
- "Purely aesthetic"

Adjacent terms to avoid: "hack", "boost" (alone), "growth-hacky", "viral", "secret formula", "best-kept secret", "next-level" (without precise context), "amazing" (always vague).

---

## 6. PERMITTED LEXICON (signal phrases)

Phrases that carry the brand correctly. Use them deliberately, not formulaically.

- "performance creative studio"
- "creative system / system architecture"
- "editorial precision"
- "asset orchestration"
- "operational clarity"
- "signature lighting"
- "Algorithm of Soul"
- "dual-city architecture"
- "release-cleared", "press-grade", "system-oriented"
- "governance layer" (when describing protocols)

---

## 7. LENGTH RULES

| Surface | Recommended length |
|---|---|
| Social caption | 1–3 sentences |
| Press contact line | 1 sentence |
| Headline | ≤ 12 words |
| Subhead | ≤ 24 words |
| Body paragraph | 3–5 sentences |
| Email response | 4–8 sentences |

If you need more length, you probably need more clarity instead.

---

## 8. STRUCTURE PRINCIPLES

1. **Lead with the system, not the campaign.** Mirrou's distinctive sound says architecture first, expression second.
2. **One claim per paragraph.** No stacked promises in a single sentence.
3. **Concrete over abstract.** "A four-quadrant asset system across 4:5, 1:1 and 9:16" beats "scalable creative."
4. **Show, don't perform.** Describe the work; don't praise it.
5. **Authority by restraint.** The strongest Mirrou voice is the one that holds back.

---

## 9. MIRROU vs OLHA — VOICE SPLIT

**Olha Yevtushenko** speaks in the first person about vision, light, intention, soul, instinct.
> "The frame is decided before the shutter. The system is decided before the frame."

**Mirrou Creative Studio** speaks in the third person about architecture, governance, performance, output.
> "Mirrou builds creative systems that survive contact with their own organisations."

Never blur the two. Olha is the eye. Mirrou is the engine.

---

## 10. MICRO-COPY EXAMPLES

**Download CTA (button):**
- Avoid: "Download now"
- Prefer: "Initiate Protocol" / "Launch System" / "Open Document"

**Empty state:**
- Avoid: "Nothing here yet."
- Prefer: "System idle. No active records."

**Error:**
- Avoid: "Oops! Something went wrong."
- Prefer: "Signal interrupted. Retry or contact `hello@mirrou.studio`."

**Status label (mono):**
- "STATUS: SYSTEMATIC ANALYSIS"
- "STATUS: GOVERNANCE LAYER ACTIVE"
- "STATUS: OUTPUT QUEUED"

---

## 11. VOICE FOR PRESS COPY

When writing for journalists:
- Open with the most editorially useful sentence (a fact, not a hook).
- Keep adjectives load-bearing — every adjective must add specific information.
- Always offer release-cleared quotes (see Founder Bio).
- Never claim figures that have not been signed off by Olha and Legal.

---

## 12. VOICE FOR SOCIAL COPY

When writing for Mirrou or @olha_yevtushenko on social:
- Default to the Personal Statement archetype on @olha_yevtushenko.
- Default to the Studio Promise archetype on @mirroustudio.
- Never use exclamation marks. Never use emoji that conflict with the gold/black/ivory palette (e.g. brightly coloured emoji). When in doubt: no emoji.
- One thought per post.
